# Narrative Hook

Phase: 01 - Concept  
Purpose: Design the return engine that makes readers keep coming back across different topics while the publication gradually educates and converts them.  
Time to Complete: 20–35 minutes

<!-- guide:narrative_hook -->

## Why This Matters

The Marty publication cannot live on correctness alone.
It needs narrative energy — not melodrama, but sustained intellectual and practical tension.

This file defines the mechanism that makes a reader think:

- I need to understand what changes if this protocol model is right
- I want to see how this specific topic fits the larger identity stack
- I suspect the identity market has been framing the problem incorrectly

Without a hook, the blog becomes a filing cabinet.
With a hook, even free-topic essays accumulate into a path.

## Current Working Draft

### Core Hook

**Hook Sentence:** Every identity system keeps solving the same five problems in incompatible ways; Marty asks what happens when we standardize those problems as one protocol instead.

**Curiosity Gap:** If standards for credentials, wallets, and verification already exist, why does identity still feel fragmented, expensive, privacy-leaking, and operationally brittle?

**Recurring Tension:** The market keeps shipping wallet features and platform APIs, while the underlying trust, disclosure, deployment, and governance problems remain unresolved.

**Why This Matters To The Reader Now:** Readers are being forced into decisions by EUDI timelines, mDL adoption, offline verification needs, and procurement pressure. They need a model before they need another vendor deck.

### Public Narrative Wrapper

**Surface Genre:** Topic-led technical publication + protocol field guide + implementation-oriented operator journal

**Narrator / Host Posture:** Specialist author bench with a shared protocol thesis underneath

**How Explicit The Teaching Is:** Contextual rather than compulsory. The guide section can signal paths, but individual posts should still work as self-contained essays.

**What The Reader Feels They Are Following:** Not a rigid class, but a growing map of the identity stack that becomes more coherent every time they return.

### Topical Engine

**What Can Change Each Installment:** The point of entry — a standard, a privacy problem, a deployment environment, a protocol primitive, a governance issue, or an implementation artifact

**What Must Stay Open Across Installments:** The master question: can Marty convert identity from a fragmented product category into interoperable infrastructure?

**Escalation Pattern Across The Publication:** Readers keep encountering the same deeper truths from different angles: platform vs protocol, policy vs code, portability vs lock-in, privacy vs overcollection, online freshness vs offline resilience

**Key Reframe To Keep Reappearing:** The hard part of identity is not just choosing a format or wallet; it is making trust, disclosure, deployment, and lifecycle behavior legible and configurable.

**Late Payoff:** The entire stack clicks: five primitives, supporting abstractions, standards mappings, deployment modes, schemas, conformance, and product packaging all read as one coherent system rather than separate topics.

### Reader Retention Design

**Why They Return For The Next Post:** Each post answers one meaningful question but reveals a deeper layer the previous explanation depended on.

**Early Payoff:** A fast collapse of the "identity is just another API category" story into something more structurally satisfying and commercially plausible

**Slow-Burn Payoff:** A durable model of the entire identity stack plus a credible reason to evaluate Marty specifically

**Question To Leave Lingering At The End Of Posts:** What becomes clearer, more portable, or more governable if this layer is standardized instead of hidden inside product code?

**CTA Rhythm:** Topic answer → adjacent concept → guide/article/spec/evaluation asset

### Hook Variants To Test

**Version A — High Urgency:** EUDI, mDL, offline verification, and new trust frameworks are arriving now. Teams that still think identity is a per-check API will make expensive architecture mistakes.

**Version B — High Mystery:** We already have wallets, standards, cryptography, and regulation — so what invisible missing layer is still preventing identity from becoming infrastructure?

**Version C — High Utility:** Learn the five primitives that let one model span passports, mDLs, enterprise badges, age verification, and education credentials.

**Version D — High Authority:** Marty organizes existing standards, deployments, and governance concerns into a formal, vendor-neutral specification with schemas, reference implementations, and conformance tests.

**Version E — High Intimacy:** If you have ever felt that modern identity conversations are half acronym soup and half vendor theater, this publication is designed to make the space finally feel understandable.

### CAP Narrative Profile Hook Semantics

Use semantic structure to keep the hook coherent across many entry points.

**Dominant Event Types:** diagnosis, revelation, comparison, implementation walkthrough, deployment pattern, synthesis

**Reader Information State Pattern:** confused → intrigued → destabilized → clarified → structured → ready to evaluate

**Causal Role Pattern:** setup → consequence → synthesis, with many posts doing both setup and consequence work for a larger Marty idea

**Primary Open Threads:** protocol vs platform, portability vs lock-in, privacy vs overcollection, declarative policy vs buried code, deployment reality vs standards rhetoric

**Reader Experience Target:** curiosity, relief, intellectual momentum, and rising confidence

### Hook Failure Modes

**What Would Make This Feel Like Clickbait?:** Repeating "identity needs a protocol" without showing the concrete primitives, artifacts, or deployment implications

**What Would Make It Feel Too Academic?:** Excessive standards jargon without business stakes, use-case grounding, or deployment implications

**What Would Make It Feel Too Generic?:** Treating Marty as just another voice in the identity discourse rather than a specific architecture and product model

**How We Keep It Honest:** Use concrete examples, keep the five primitives visible, tie ideas back to the live guide/spec/artifacts, and make the commercial handoff explicit without turning every post into product copy.
